# Video Script: The Redpath Mansion Mystery

**(VOICEOVER - Narrator: Calm, investigative, serious tone)**

In the early 20th century, the Redpath family was Canadian royalty. Their fortune, built on sugar and steel, shaped the city of Montreal. Their mansion was a palace, a symbol of immense power and influence.

But on June 13th, 1901, that empire of wealth was shattered by two gunshots.

The official story would be written in less than 48 hours. The case closed before it was ever truly opened. But the truth... was buried with the bodies.

This is the report of the Redpath Mansion Mystery.

**(MUSIC CUE: Ominous, slow-building introductory theme)**

### The Official Story

On the evening of June 13th, 1901, gunshots echoed through the Redpath family's mansion on Sherbrooke Street. Ada Redpath, the 59-year-old matriarch of the family, was found dead in her bedroom. Beside her lay her 24-year-old son, Clifford.

Ada had been shot in the back of the head. Clifford, in the left temple.

The family's physician, Dr. Thomas Roddick, was summoned to the scene. The very next day, a coroner's inquest was convened, not in a public courthouse, but inside the mansion itself.

The verdict was swift and decisive. The coroner declared that Clifford, in a state of temporary insanity brought on by an epileptic seizure, had shot his mother before turning the gun on himself. A tragic, but closed, case of murder-suicide.

The family, seeking to preserve its dignity, buried Ada and Clifford within 48 hours. The story was sealed.

But the official report is not the only story. When you look closer, the facts begin to unravel, revealing a series of inconsistencies that point not to a simple tragedy, but to a potential cover-up at the highest levels of society.

### The Contradictions

Let's examine the evidence that casts doubt on the official narrative.

**First: The Police Were Never Called.**
In a case involving two violent deaths, the Montreal police were never summoned to investigate. The entire affair was handled internally by the family and their personal physician. This is a staggering departure from any standard procedure, then or now.

**Second: The Hasty Inquest.**
The coroner's inquest was rushed. Held the very next day inside the home, with a jury of prominent citizens, it relied on a few select testimonies and reached a conclusion in record time. There was no external investigation.

**Third: The Missing Suicide Record.**
Perhaps the most damning piece of evidence against the official story is a simple clerical fact. City records for Montreal in June of 1901 show no reported suicides. If Clifford Redpath had taken his own life, it was never officially filed as such, directly contradicting the coroner's public verdict.

**Fourth: Conflicting Timelines.**
Key testimonies and reports don't align. Some newspapers reported that Dr. Roddick, the primary medical witness, was not even in Montreal on the day of the deaths and didn't return until the following morning. Hospital records show no sign of Clifford ever being admitted, despite some accounts claiming he was taken there.

**Fifth: The Wall of Silence.**
The family's explanation rested entirely on Clifford's epilepsy. Yet, prior to the tragedy, there is no mention of this condition in any known family documents or letters. The story of his illness, so crucial to the official verdict, seems to appear only after his death.

### Conclusion

So what truly happened in the Redpath Mansion?

Was it a tragic murder-suicide, hastily covered up by a powerful family to avoid the public stigma of epilepsy and suicide?

Or was it something more sinister? A double murder, disguised and dismissed by a coroner and a city elite loyal to one of their own?

With the police barred from investigating, and the primary evidence buried within two days, the truth became impossible to find. The mansion was demolished in 1956, erasing the crime scene forever.

Over a century later, the official story remains the only one on record. But the facts of the case tell a different tale. They speak of secrets, power, and a mystery that continues to move in the darkness, just beyond our reach.
