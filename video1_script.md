# Video Script: "The Midnight Signal"

**(SCENE START)**

**(VOICEOVER - Calm, slow, measured)**

It was old, even by my standards. A Kenwood ham radio, bought from a dead man’s estate sale. All polished wood and brushed steel, with a main dial that glowed a sickly, jaundiced yellow in the dark of my workshop.

For months, it was just a novelty. I’d scan the bands, listening to the lonely chatter of truckers and the strange, rhythmic pulses of numbers stations from shores I’d never see.

But last Tuesday, something new appeared.

It was just past midnight. I was about to turn the machine off when I caught it. A faint signal, hiding behind the static on an unused frequency. It was a simple, repeating tone. A single, electronic note, holding for three seconds, then silence for ten.

*(Sound of a faint, clean electronic tone, followed by static)*

Three seconds on. Ten seconds off. Over and over.

It wasn't a numbers station. It wasn't Morse code. It had no discernible pattern or purpose. It was just… there. An electronic ghost.

I became obsessed. Every night, I’d come down to the workshop, tune the dial to that exact frequency, and listen. The signal was always there. Unwavering. A digital heartbeat in the void.

For three days, it never changed. But on the fourth night, it did.

The silence between the tones shortened. From ten seconds, to nine. Then eight. I sat there, my hand frozen on the dial, as the silence compressed, second by second.

*(Sound of the tone, with the silence between each beep shortening progressively)*

The rhythm became urgent. A frantic, silent countdown.

Then, just as the silence reached zero, the tone stopped.

A new sound crackled through the speaker. A voice. Dry, like rustling leaves. It spoke only a single sentence, in a language that felt ancient and wrong. And as it spoke, the yellow dial on the Kenwood flickered, and went dark.

I don’t know what the words meant. But I know what they were.

They were a reply.

And they weren't meant for me.

**(SCENE END)**
