Quick Answer: In most professional settings, the business logo or brand image is placed within the email signature itself, usually alongside or just below the sender’s name and contact details. It’s rarely placed above the signature, since the logo is meant to reinforce brand identity at the close of the message.

Best Practices for Logo Placement in Business Email Signatures

    Within the signature block

        The logo is typically integrated into the signature, appearing next to or beneath the sender’s name, title, and contact information.

        This ensures the recipient associates the sender directly with the company brand.

Many businesses place the logo at the bottom of the signature, after the name, title, phone number, and email address.

This keeps the focus on the sender’s identity first, then reinforces the company brand.

Beside the sender’s details

    Some designs position the logo to the left or right of the text block, creating a balanced, professional look.

    This is common in modern, mobile-friendly templates.


Why Below Works Better Than Above

    Clarity: Putting the logo above the signature can look cluttered or distract from the sender’s name and role.

    Brand reinforcement: Ending with the logo ties the message back to the company, leaving a lasting impression.

    Consistency: Most email signature generators and corporate templates default to placing logos within or below the signature block.
