# Module 4: Developing Your Core Design Collection

This module is the creative core of your business. We'll move from the abstract idea of a "niche" to producing a tangible, cohesive, and desirable collection of designs. A strong brand isn't built on random successes; it's built on a consistent and recognizable style.

---

## 1. Exercise: The Style Mood Board & Lexicon

Before you generate a single image, you must define your visual identity.

**Action Step 1: Create a Style Mood Board.**
Use a tool like Pinterest or simply a folder on your computer. Gather 15-20 images that represent the feeling you want your brand to evoke. Don't just collect images *of* your subject matter; collect images for their *style*. Look for color palettes, line quality, texture, and overall composition. Is your brand dark and moody? Light and whimsical? Clean and modern? Your mood board is your visual anchor.

**Action Step 2: Create Your Style Lexicon.**
Look at your mood board and translate the visuals into words. These words will become the foundation of your prompts. Create a text file and list 5-10 keywords that define your style. This is your "Style Lexicon."

*   **Example Lexicon for a "Vintage Sci-Fi" Niche:**
    *   `retro-futurism`
    *   `vintage comic book style`
    *   `halftone pattern`
    *   `aged paper texture`
    *   `limited color palette`
    *   `bold outlines`
    *   `1950s aesthetic`

This lexicon is now the "DNA" of your brand's visual style.

---

## 2. The "Hero, Secondary, Pattern" Strategy

A robust design collection has variety. It allows you to cater to different products and price points. We will use a three-tiered approach:

*   **Hero Designs (2-3 per collection):** These are your masterpieces. Complex, detailed scenes that tell a story. They are perfect for high-margin products like posters, canvases, or the main feature on a t-shirt. They are your "wow" pieces.
    *   *Example (Vintage Sci-Fi):* A full scene of a rocket ship landing on a mysterious alien planet with two astronauts exploring.

*   **Secondary Designs (4-6 per collection):** These are simpler, iconic graphics, often featuring a single subject. They are incredibly versatile and work perfectly on mugs, hats, stickers, and as smaller chest-logos on apparel. They are easy for customers to understand at a glance.
    *   *Example (Vintage Sci-Fi):* A stylized ray-gun, a single retro astronaut helmet, a simple rocket ship silhouette.

*   **Pattern Designs (2-3 per collection):** These are seamless, repeating patterns made from the elements in your secondary designs. They are essential for all-over-print products like phone cases, tote bags, wrapping paper, or as background textures.
    *   *Example (Vintage Sci-Fi):* A repeating pattern of small ray-guns and stars.

---

## 3. Prompting for a Cohesive Collection: The "Style Clause"

To ensure your collection looks like it came from a single, unified brand, we will use a "Style Clause." This is a reusable chunk of your prompt that contains the keywords from your Style Lexicon.

**Action Step: Build Your Style Clause.**
Combine your lexicon keywords into a single string. You will append this to almost every prompt you write.

*   **Example Style Clause (Vintage Sci-Fi):**
    `... in a retro-futurism vintage comic book style, halftone pattern, aged paper texture, limited color palette, 1950s aesthetic`

**Putting It All Together (Secondary Design Example):**

**Positive Prompt:**
`a stylized retro ray-gun, simple, iconic, isolated on a white background, in a retro-futurism vintage comic book style, halftone pattern, aged paper texture, limited color palette, 1950s aesthetic`

**Negative Prompt:**
`photorealistic, 3d render, modern, complex, human, text, watermark`

By using this Style Clause consistently, you force the AI to generate images that share the same visual DNA, resulting in a professional and cohesive collection.

---

## 4. Professional Asset Management

A creative business runs on its assets. Keep them organized from day one.

*   **File Naming Convention:** Use a clear and consistent naming scheme.
    `Theme_Design-Type_Subject_Version.png`
    *   `Vintage-Sci-Fi_Secondary_Ray-Gun_v01.png`
    *   `Vintage-Sci-Fi_Hero_Alien-Planet-Landing_v01.png`
    *   `Vintage-Sci-Fi_Pattern_Stars-and-Guns_v01.png`

*   **Prompt Log:** Keep a spreadsheet or text file. For each file, log the exact positive and negative prompts used to create it. This is invaluable for recreating or iterating on a successful design later.

This structured approach to creativity is what separates hobbyists from professional brand builders. By completing these steps, you will have a powerful, versatile, and monetizable collection of core designs.
